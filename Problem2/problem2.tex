\subsection*{Problem 2.1}

An operator $\hat{H}$ is represented in a particular orthonormal basis
as the matrix
\begin{equation}
\hat{H}\simeq
\begin{pmatrix}
1 & \frac{i}{2} & 0 \\
-\frac{i}{2} & 1 & 0 \\
0 & 0 & \frac{1}{2}
\end{pmatrix}
\end{equation}

\subsubsection*{a)}

$\hat{H}$ is Hermitian if it is equal to its own transpose conjugate,
$\hat{H}=\hat{H}^{\dagger}$.

\begin{equation}
\hat{H}^{\dagger}=(\hat{H}^*)^T=
\begin{pmatrix}
1 & -\frac{i}{2} & 0 \\
\frac{i}{2} & 1 & 0 \\
0 & 0 & \frac{1}{2}
\end{pmatrix}^T=
\begin{pmatrix}
1 & \frac{i}{2} & 0 \\
-\frac{i}{2} & 1 & 0 \\
0 & 0 & \frac{1}{2}
\end{pmatrix}=
\hat{H}
\end{equation}

\subsection*{b)}

Three ket vectors are given 
\begin{align}
\ket{1} \simeq& \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}\colvec[i]{1}{0} \\
\ket{2} \simeq& \colvec[0]{0}{1} \\
\ket{3} \simeq& \frac{1}{\sqrt{3}}\colvec[-i]{1}{-1} \\
\end{align}
